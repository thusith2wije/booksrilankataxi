Most visited places in Galle

Galle Fort

Galle Fort

Explore the historic Dutch-built fort and its stunning ocean views. A UNESCO World Heritage site, Galle Fort is known for its colonial architecture, boutique shops, and charming streets.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach

Relax on one of Sri Lanka’s most famous beaches with golden sand. Unawatuna Beach is a tropical paradise perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Jungle Beach

Jungle Beach

A hidden gem with crystal-clear waters and lush greenery. A secluded and serene beach, ideal for snorkeling and escaping the crowds.

Japanese Peace Pagoda

Japanese Peace Pagoda

A white stupa offering panoramic views of the coastline. Located on a hill, the Peace Pagoda is a tranquil spot with breathtaking sunsets.

Galle Lighthouse

Galle Lighthouse

Sri Lanka’s oldest lighthouse, offering postcard-perfect views. An iconic landmark standing tall on the Galle Fort walls, overlooking the Indian Ocean.

Sea Turtle Hatchery

Sea Turtle Hatchery

Visit a conservation center dedicated to protecting sea turtles. Learn about turtle conservation efforts and even release baby turtles into the sea.

Galle National Museum

Galle National Museum

Discover Galle’s rich history through artifacts and exhibits. This museum showcases colonial-era relics, traditional masks, and ancient artifacts.

Dutch Reformed Church

Dutch Reformed Church

Admire this 18th-century church with stunning architecture. One of the oldest Protestant churches in Sri Lanka, featuring unique tombstones and stained glass windows.

Explore the Historic Beauty of Galle

Galle, a coastal city in southern Sri Lanka, is a must-visit destination known for its colonial charm, golden beaches, and cultural heritage. As the home of the iconic Galle Fort, this UNESCO World Heritage site is filled with cobbled streets, Dutch-era buildings, and lively cafés that transport you back in time. Whether you're a history buff, a beach lover, or an adventure seeker, Galle offers something for everyone.
Beyond the fort, Galle boasts pristine beaches such as Unawatuna and Jungle Beach, where you can unwind under the tropical sun or take part in thrilling water activities like snorkeling and diving. The city is also home to remarkable landmarks like the Japanese Peace Pagoda, the Galle Lighthouse, and the famous Sea Turtle Hatchery, where visitors can witness conservation efforts firsthand.

Galle is not just about sightseeing; it's also a hub for delicious seafood, vibrant markets, and a thriving arts scene. Stroll through the local boutiques, visit the Galle National Museum, or experience the rich culture by exploring ancient mosques and churches that add to the city's unique character.
