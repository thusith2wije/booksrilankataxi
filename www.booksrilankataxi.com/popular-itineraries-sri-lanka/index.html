Southwest Monsoon (May - September):

Brings heavy rainfall to the southwestern and central regions, affecting Colombo, Galle, and Kandy.

National Parks in Sri Lanka

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is Sri Lanka’s most famous wildlife sanctuary, known for having one of the highest leopard densities in the world. Explore its vast landscapes of dry forests, grasslands, and lagoons while spotting elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and a variety of exotic birds. A safari in Yala promises thrilling encounters with nature in its wildest form.

Udawalawe National Park

Udawalawe is the go-to destination for elephant lovers. Home to a large population of wild elephants, the park offers incredible up-close sightings, especially around the Udawalawe Reservoir. The open plains and scenic backdrops also make it ideal for photography and birdwatching adventures.

Wilpattu National Park

As Sri Lanka’s largest and oldest national park, Wilpattu is famous for its unique “willus” – natural lakes that attract wildlife throughout the year. The park is a serene escape for those wanting to spot leopards, elephants, and deer in a BookSriLankaTaxi crowded, more tranquil setting.

Horton Plains National Park

Located in the central highlands, Horton Plains is a misty paradise with breathtaking landscapes, cloud forests, and rolling grasslands. Its star attraction, World’s End, is a dramatic cliff offering stunning views. The park is also home to rare wildlife like the sambar deer and unique bird species.

Minneriya National Park

Minneriya is renowned for the spectacular “Gathering” – the seasonal congregation of hundreds of elephants near the Minneriya Tank. This wildlife phenomenon is a must-see, alongside sightings of monkeys, deer, and colorful birds in this lush, historic park.

Bundala National Park

A haven for birdwatchers, Bundala National Park is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve famous for its rich wetlands and migratory birds, including flamingos and storks. The park’s coastal lagoons, sand dunes, and salt pans also support crocodiles, elephants, and more, offering a peaceful yet wild experience.
