Most visited places in Kandy

Temple of the Tooth Relic

Temple of the Tooth Relic

A sacred Buddhist temple housing the relic of Buddha’s tooth. This UNESCO-listed site is one of the most revered Buddhist shrines in the world.

Peradeniya Botanical Gardens

Peradeniya Botanical Gardens

Sri Lanka’s largest botanical garden, home to exotic flora. Famous for its orchid house, giant bamboo, and centuries-old trees.

Kandy Lake

Kandy Lake

A scenic lake in the heart of Kandy, perfect for a peaceful stroll. Surrounded by greenery and historical sites, offering breathtaking sunset views.

Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue

Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue

A towering Buddha statue overlooking the city of Kandy. A short hike offers panoramic city views and a tranquil atmosphere.

Udawattakele Forest Reserve

Udawattakele Forest Reserve

A lush forest sanctuary with walking trails and wildlife. A great spot for birdwatching and escaping the city's hustle.

Ceylon Tea Museum

Ceylon Tea Museum

Learn about Sri Lanka’s rich tea heritage and taste authentic Ceylon tea. This museum showcases old tea-processing machines and the history of Ceylon tea.

Kandy Viewpoint

Kandy Viewpoint

A spectacular viewpoint offering breathtaking views of the city. Best visited at sunrise or sunset for the most scenic experience.

Lankatilaka Temple

Lankatilaka Temple

A stunning 14th-century temple with intricate Kandyan architecture. A cultural gem showcasing impressive Buddhist and Hindu influences.

Embekke Devalaya

Embekke Devalaya

A historic temple known for its intricate wooden carvings. A masterpiece of Sri Lankan architecture, built during the Gampola era.

Kandy Cultural Show

Kandy Cultural Show

Experience traditional Sri Lankan dance performances. A vibrant show featuring fire-walking, drumming, and Kandyan dances.

Discover the Cultural Heart of Sri Lanka – Kandy

Kandy, the cultural capital of Sri Lanka, is a city rich in history, religion, and breathtaking landscapes. Nestled among rolling hills, this UNESCO-listed city is home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic, one of the most important Buddhist sites in the world. Whether you're interested in exploring cultural heritage, scenic nature, or vibrant local traditions, Kandy has something for every traveler.
The city's heart is Kandy Lake, offering a peaceful escape amidst bustling streets. Nearby, the Peradeniya Botanical Gardens showcase the island’s rich biodiversity, making it a must-visit for nature lovers. For those looking for adventure, hiking up to the Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue or exploring the Udawattakele Forest Reserve provides breathtaking views and a serene atmosphere.

Kandy is also the gateway to Sri Lanka’s famous tea plantations. A visit to the Ceylon Tea Museum gives insight into the history of tea production, with opportunities to sample some of the finest brews. As the sun sets, the city comes alive with cultural shows featuring traditional Kandyan dance and fire-walking performances, offering a glimpse into Sri Lanka’s rich artistic heritage. With a mix of spiritual, cultural, and natural attractions, Kandy is a city that deserves to be explored at your own pace.
