Explore Kalutara – A Coastal & Cultural Gem

Kalutara, located along Sri Lanka’s southwest coast, is a fascinating blend of beachside relaxation and cultural heritage. The city is most famous for the Kalutara Bodhiya, a sacred Buddhist temple known for its impressive stupa and riverside setting. It’s one of the most revered religious sites in the country, attracting thousands of visitors each year. For those interested in colonial history, Richmond Castle stands as a magnificent reminder of the country’s past, featuring elegant architecture and lush gardens. Beach lovers will enjoy Kalutara Beach, where the golden sands and calm waters create the perfect setting for unwinding under the sun.

Nature enthusiasts can take a short drive to Thudugala Ella Waterfall, a serene escape surrounded by dense forest, ideal for a refreshing swim. Meanwhile, adventurers can explore Fa Hien Cave, an archaeological wonder that dates back thousands of years and holds evidence of early human settlements. Whether you're looking to soak in the sun, explore cultural landmarks, or dive into Sri Lanka’s rich history, Kalutara has something for every traveler.
