Discover the Wild Beauty of Udawalawa

Udawalawa is a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. Located in the southern region of Sri Lanka, this destination is best known for its famous Udawalawa National Park, where visitors can embark on thrilling safaris to spot elephants, leopards, and exotic birds in their natural habitat. The park’s vast grasslands and water bodies provide a spectacular backdrop for unforgettable wildlife encounters. Beyond the safari experience, Udawalawa is home to the Elephant Transit Home, a conservation project dedicated to rescuing and rehabilitating orphaned elephants. Visitors can watch these gentle giants being fed and cared for before they are released back into the wild.

For those looking to explore beyond the wildlife, Udawalawa offers picturesque landscapes, including the stunning Udawalawa Reservoir, ideal for birdwatching and photography. History enthusiasts can visit the Maduwanwela Walawwa, a colonial-era mansion showcasing Sri Lanka’s aristocratic past, while adventure seekers can venture into the Waulpane Limestone Cave, featuring an underground waterfall and unique rock formations.
