Top Attractions in Haputale

Lipton's Seat

Lipton's Seat

Enjoy breathtaking panoramic views from this iconic viewpoint. One of Sri Lanka’s most famous lookouts, Lipton’s Seat offers a stunning sunrise over tea plantations.

Adisham Bungalow

Adisham Bungalow

A charming colonial mansion surrounded by lush gardens. This historic bungalow, once a British governor’s residence, now serves as a monastery and visitor attraction.

Dambatenne Tea Factory

Dambatenne Tea Factory

Experience tea processing at a renowned tea estate. Tour this working tea factory and learn about the journey from leaf to cup.

Bambarakanda Falls

Bambarakanda Falls

Sri Lanka’s tallest waterfall, cascading from 263 meters. A mesmerizing natural wonder, perfect for hiking and photography.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains National Park

Explore diverse wildlife and visit the famous World's End cliff. This highland plateau offers misty landscapes, scenic trails, and breathtaking drop-offs.

St. Andrew’s Church

St. Andrew’s Church

A historic church with stunning architecture and serene surroundings. Built during British rule, this church reflects Haputale’s colonial past.

Thangamale Bird Sanctuary

Thangamale Bird Sanctuary

A paradise for bird watchers and nature lovers. This sanctuary is home to a variety of exotic birds and offers scenic trails through lush greenery.

Discover the Beauty of Haputale

Nestled in Sri Lanka’s misty hill country, Haputale is a hidden gem known for its lush tea plantations, colonial heritage, and breathtaking viewpoints. This small town offers a peaceful retreat away from bustling cities, with cool mountain air and panoramic vistas that stretch for miles. Haputale’s most famous attraction, Lipton’s Seat, offers visitors a chance to stand where Sir Thomas Lipton once surveyed his tea empire. The surrounding plantations and Dambatenne Tea Factory provide an immersive experience into Sri Lanka’s tea culture. For history lovers, Adisham Bungalow and St. Andrew’s Church showcase British colonial architecture and tranquil gardens.

Beyond tea and history, Haputale boasts incredible natural attractions like Bambarakanda Falls, the tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka, and Horton Plains National Park, home to the dramatic World’s End cliff. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing escape, Haputale has something to offer. Exploring Haputale is best done at your own pace.
